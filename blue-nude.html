Around the time Matisse gained stomach cancer, he started doing cutout pieces

Matisse before was an impressionist artist, such as with his first Blue Nude (1907)

Matisse was also a founder in Fauvism, which prioritized color, especially using color straight out of the tube.

His journey in rejecting the canon European art and more into his "Modern" art, Matisse took inspiration from his African sculpture.

From his trip to the Tahitian islands in 1930 and his revelation in Islamic art, Matisse was inspired into crating his cut out pieceâ€”specifically, the shapes and lineless forms replicated from Polynesian and Islam art.

His process with cutouts started out with painting a big paper canvas with one color, straight out of the tube

He then (with the help of his assistants) cut out fluid and organic shapes with scissors

With pins, he placed the cut out shapes onto a bigger paper canvas, repeating the process until he was satisfied.
